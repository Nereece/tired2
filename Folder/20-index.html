Trends of the 20s

The prohibition in the US, along with the emerge of feminism and independence for women inspired fashion designs with comfort, practicality and style in mind. Women no longer wanted to wear corsets and constricting long dresses. Instead they opted for 'Flapper Fashion' a style that is still well known a century later. Consisting of looser dresses with knee length hemlines and a dropped waistline, creating a 'boyish looking figure'. The dresses would have a variety of embellishments including beading, fringes and feathers. Paired with heavier and darker makeup, accessories and many women also adopted a short bob haircut.

Current trends taken from this era:

Coco Channel envisioned the Little Black Dress 1926 as a simple and fashionable dress for all women and inspired "fashionable" women's trousers while wearing a pair herself on holiday in Deauville. Other trends taken from this era include long pleated skirts and short tennis skirts, as women became more interested in doing sports such as tennis and golf in the 1920's. Drop waist dresses and made to move silhouettes for dancing as well as embellishments on garments such as beads, pearls, tiny crystals, fringes and feathers.
