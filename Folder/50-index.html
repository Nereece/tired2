1950's

Trends of the 50s

The trend that dominated this decade was given the name 'New Look'. Designed by Christian Dior, this trend was the complete opposite to what women wore in the 40s. As the war was over expensive materials were being used again to design a look similar to the mid-nineteenth century. With women having to wear boned corsets, bust and hip pads to create the perfect hourglass figure. Although it was acceptable for women to wear casual clothing at home, they were expected to look perfect in public and when their husbands arrived home from work. This look consisted of huge skirts at mid-calf length with softer shoulders, unlike the padding used during the 40s. Women's appearances were linked to how successful their husbands were even if they weren't well off women were pressured to appear this way. By having expensive dresses with accessories that all matched perfectly with their outfits.

Women's thoughts

Many women were outraged, especially in the United States. Protests were held targeting Dior and his new clothing. Women had just gained a large amount of equality and weren’t ready to give it up — both in work and in fashion. The new clothes used excessive amounts of fabric, needed constant maintenance, and required a complete coordinated accessory collection to be “perfect.” However, after the hardships of the war, everybody was ready for a change, and by the start of the ‘50s, everybody was wearing the New Look.

Coco Chanel disliked the ‘New Look’ so much that she reopened her business after closing it at the start of the war. In 1954, she came back with slim suits – the brand’s signature look – in wools and tweeds. Jackets were boxy with no collar, and skirts were straight and comfortable. She topped the look off with costume jewellery and the famous quilted bag.
